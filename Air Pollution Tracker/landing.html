Causes of Air Pollution

Air pollution is caused by solid and liquid particles and certain gases that are suspended in the air. These particles and gases can come from car and truck exhaust, factories, dust, pollen, volcanoes and wildfires. The solid and liquid particles suspended in our air are called aerosols. Air pollution happens when aerosols and certain gases end up in our air. These particles and gases can be bad for the planet and for our health, so keeping track of them is important.

    • The Burning of Fossil Fuels

    Most of the air pollution takes place due to the burning of fossil fuels such as coal, oil, gasoline to produce energy for electricity or transportation. The release of carbon monoxide in high level indicates how much fossil fuel is burned. This also emits other toxic pollutants in the air. Sulphur dioxide emitted from the combustion of fossil fuels like coal, petroleum for energy in power plants, and other factory combustibles is one the major cause of air pollution.

    Exhaust from Factories and Industries

    Industrial activities emit several pollutants in the air that affects the air quality more than we can even imagine. Manufacturing industries release a large amount of carbon monoxide, hydrocarbons, organic compounds, and chemicals into the air, thereby depleting the quality of air. Particulate matter 2.5 and 10, Nitrogen dioxide, Sulphur dioxide, and carbon monoxide are key pollutants that are emitted from industries that use coal and wood as their primary energy source for production of their goods.

    Transportation

    There is no denying that vehicle pollution is the major contributor to air pollution, especially in urban cities. When the car burns gasoline, it emits pollutants in the air which is as harmful as smoking 10 cigarettes a day. Your vehicle emits carbon monoxide, hydrocarbons, nitrogen oxide, and particulate matter. When the vehicle pollution is high in the atmosphere, it creates a hole in the ozone layer contributing to smog and causing various health issues.

    Wildfires

    Climate change is not just increasing wildfire but also spiking air pollution. Burning stubble and farm residue is also a major contribution to wildfire. It causes increased PM 2.5 in the air which collides with other harmful substances like chemical gas and pollen creating smog. Smog makes the air hazy and people find it difficult to breathe.

    Microbial Decaying Process

    Manufacturing, chemical, and textiles industries release a large number of carbon monoxides, hydrocarbons, chemicals and organic compounds which contaminate our environment. Bacteria and fungi play a fundamental role in the biogeochemical cycles in nature. Decaying of these microorganisms present in the surrounding releases methane gas which is highly toxic. Landfills are land areas in which waste is deposited or buried. These deposited or buried wastes generate methane. Methane is a major greenhouse gas that is highly flammable and very hazardous.

    Agricultural Activities

    Agricultural activities have had a serious impact on the decreasing air quality. To begin with pesticides and fertilizers are the main source to contaminate the surrounding air. Ammonia is a very common byproduct of agriculture-related activities and is one of the most hazardous gases in the atmosphere. The use of insecticides, pesticides, and fertilizers in agricultural activities has grown quite a lot. They emit harmful chemicals into the air. Farmers also set the field and old crops on fire in order to keep them clean for the next round of sowing. The burning to clean fields is said to cause pollution by releasing harmful gases in the air.

    Open Burning of Garbage Waste

    Open burning of garbage is much more harmful to your health and the environment than one may think. Exposure to open burning of garbage waste can pose serious health risk including cancer, liver issues, impairment of immune system, reproductive functions; can also affect the developing nervous system.

    Indoor Air Pollution

    Talking about air pollution, we always consider outdoor air pollution dangerous for our lives but never talk about indoor air pollution. Household products cause indoor air pollution which is 10 times more harmful than outdoor air pollution. Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) found in paints, cleaners and personal care products such as perfume and deodorants are a reason for common heath issues. Risks like asthma or other respiratory issues and lung disease are other issues cause by inhaling poor house air quality.

    Natural Events

    There are certain natural events such as volcanoes, forest fires, and dust storms, which are natural born and causes Air Pollution.

    • Effects of air pollution

    Like people, animals, and plants, entire ecosystems can suffer effects from air pollution. Haze, like smog, is a visible type of air pollution that obscures shapes and colors. Hazy air pollution can even muffle sounds. Air pollution particles eventually fall back to Earth. Air pollution can directly contaminate the surface of bodies of water and soil. This can kill crops or reduce their yield. It can kill young trees and other plants. Sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxide particles in the air, can create acid rain when they mix with water and oxygen in the atmosphere. These air pollutants come mostly from coal-fired power plants and motor vehicles. When acid rain falls to Earth, it damages plants by changing soil composition; degrades water quality in rivers, lakes and streams; damages crops; and can cause buildings and monuments to decay. Like humans, animals can suffer health effects from exposure to air pollution. Birth defects, diseases, and lower reproductive rates have all been attributed to air pollution.

    How to Reduce Air Pollution

    There are ways to prevent, control and eventually reduce air pollution.In addition, monitoring air pollution levels has become very important to detect pollution peaks, better control air pollution and eventually improve air quality.There are numerous pollution control devices which have been developed to reduce the pollution. They are being extensively used in industries. Though, they have their limitations still they are helping the industrial world to reduce pollution to some extent.

    • Renewable fuel and clean energy production

    The most basic solution for air pollution is to move away from fossil fuels, replacing them with alternative energies like solar, wind and geothermal.

    Energy conservation and efficiency

    Producing clean energy is crucial. But equally important is to reduce our consumption of energy by adopting responsible habits and using more efficient devices.

    Eco-friendly transportation

    Shifting to electric vehicles and hydrogen vehicles, and promoting shared mobility (i.e carpooling, and public transports) could reduce air pollution.Alternative fuels include bio-diesel, bio-alcohol (methanol, ethanol, butane) and other biomass sources.

    Cyclone separator

    Cyclones—also referred to as cyclone dust collectors—are air pollution control devices which, similarly to air filters, separate dry particulate matter from gaseous emissions.

    Its efficiency is 50-99%.It is low cost.It suitables for 10-100µm particles.

    Wet scrubber(Venturi scrubber)

    A wet scrubber or wet scrubber system is one type of scrubber that is used to remove harmful materials from industrial exhaust gases—known as flue gas—before they are released into the environment. It was the original type of scrubbing system, and utilizes a wet substance to remove acidic gases that contribute to acid rain.

    Its efficiency is 95-99%. It is also low capital cost. It is suitable for 2.5-10µm particles.

    Electrostatic Precipitators

    Electrostatic precipitators (ESPs), like air filters and cyclones, are air pollution control devices used to collect and remove particulate matter, such as dust, from industrial emissions and exhaust.

    Its efficiency is 99% for PM10 and 68-95% for PM2.5. It is also relatively low cost. It is suitable for 0.02-2.5µm particles.

    Dry scrubber

    A dry scrubber or dry scrubber system is one type of scrubber that is used to remove harmful materials from industrial exhaust gases before they are released into the environment. Dry scrubbers are the type most commonly used in plants today, and they utilize a collection of dry substances to remove acidic gases that contribute to acid rain. Dry scrubbers are used mainly to remove acid gases from combustion sources.

    It is just like wet scrubber. It is more preferable than wet scrubber due to low wastage generation.

    Artificial Tree

    Another great modern innovation is the artificial tree. These “super trees” intake 200,000 cubic meters of polluted air each day, expelling only pure oxygen in exchange. This is done through the innovative and creative use of a water filtration system.

    It is equivalent to 250-350 natural trees. It removes 240 tons of carbon dioxide per year. It is easy to maintain. It is highly expensive.

    • Steps Taken By Government

    IImplementation of Odd-Even Rule: The government has periodically enforced an odd-even rule for vehicles in the Kathmandu Valley. This rule restricts vehicles with specific license plate numbers from being on the road on certain days of the week. The aim is to reduce traffic congestion and air pollution.

    Promotion of Electric Vehicles (EVs): The government has been encouraging the adoption of electric vehicles by providing incentives such as tax breaks and subsidies for EV buyers. This move aims to reduce emissions from traditional fossil-fuel-powered vehicles.

    Vehicle Emission Standards: Nepal has been working on aligning its vehicle emission standards with international norms to reduce vehicle emissions. This includes stricter regulations on vehicle emissions and the import of vehicles meeting these standards.

    Public Transportation Improvement: Efforts have been made to improve public transportation in the Kathmandu Valley, including the introduction of new bus services and the expansion of the existing fleet of electric buses.

    Monitoring and Data Collection: The government has invested in air quality monitoring stations across the Kathmandu Valley to collect real-time data on air quality. This data is used to assess the severity of air pollution and make informed decisions.

    Promotion of Clean Energy: There is a push to promote clean energy sources such as liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) for cooking and heating to reduce reliance on biomass fuels, which contribute significantly to indoor air pollution.

    Regulation of Construction Activities: The government has imposed regulations on construction activities to control dust pollution. These regulations include requirements for dust control measures at construction sites.

    Awareness Campaigns: The government, along with various non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and civil society, has conducted awareness campaigns to educate the public about the adverse effects of air pollution and encourage sustainable practices.

    Legislation and Policy Development: Nepal has been working on developing comprehensive air quality management policies and legislation to address air pollution effectively. These policies encompass various sectors contributing to pollution, including transportation, industry, and construction.
